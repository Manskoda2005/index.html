1. Bioscience Problem: Tempe Fermentation Optimization

In Malaysia's food security context, traditional fermentation like Tempe production relies heavily on the growth rate of Rhizopus oligosporus. Monitoring this growth is crucial; if fermentation is stopped too early, the soybeans won't bind. If left too long, spoilage occurs. By using derivatives ($f'(x)$), food technologists can determine the exact hour when the growth rate peaks (inflection point) to optimize processing efficiency and ensure food safety.

Data Reference Source: Realistic parameters adapted from Malaysian food science standards on solid-state soy fermentation profiles.

Growth Function, $f(x)$

Represents the biomass percentage (%) over time $x$ (hours):

f(x) = 100 / (1 + 9e^(-0.5x))

Derivative Function, $f'(x)$

Represents the instantaneous growth rate (% per hour):

f'(x) = 0.5 * f(x) * (1 - f(x)/100)
